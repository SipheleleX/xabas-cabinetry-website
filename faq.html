Materials & quality

We offer a range of quality materials to suit different budgets and aesthetics: melamine board (durable, easy to clean, available in a wide range of solid colours and wood-grain finishes), wood grain finishes (realistic wood appearance at affordable prices), and high gloss options for a more contemporary look.

All installations include soft-close European hinges and drawer runners as standard. All materials meet industry standards and come with supplier warranties.

Yes. We offer a wide range of colours and finishes. During your site visit, we'll bring material samples so you can see the options in your actual space with your lighting — that makes all the difference. Custom colour matching is also available for certain materials.

Yes — soft-close hinges and drawer runners are included as standard in every installation. They're more durable, operate smoothly, and eliminate slamming. It's not an optional extra with us.

Melamine comes in solid colours (white, grey, black, and many more). Very durable, easy to clean, and the most affordable option. Wood grain uses a realistic wood-look finish on a melamine base — same durability, slightly warmer aesthetic, marginally higher cost. Both are excellent choices; it comes down to your style preference and budget.

Daily care: Wipe with a damp cloth and mild detergent, then dry immediately. Never leave standing water on surfaces or around edges. Avoid abrasive cleaners, harsh chemicals, or bleach. Avoid direct heat exposure. With proper care, your cabinetry will look great for many years.

Measurements & design

You can provide rough measurements to give us a general idea, but we always take professional measurements during the site visit. Walls are rarely perfectly straight, ceilings can vary in height, and there are often hidden obstacles that affect the design. Accurate measurements are critical — mistakes are expensive to fix.

Yes. After the site visit, we include a basic layout drawing with your quote showing the general configuration. Detailed technical drawings are finalised once you approve the quote and pay the deposit.

Installation process

We take cleanliness seriously. Dust sheets are used to protect floors and surroundings throughout the job. At the end of each working day, we do a full site cleanup. On completion, we do a thorough cleanup including vacuuming and waste removal. There will be some dust and disruption — that's unavoidable — but we minimise it and clean up properly.

Not for the entire time. On the first day, it's good to be present. During the rest of the project, someone should provide access and be available for any questions. On the final day, you must be present for the inspection and sign-off. If you can't be home, a responsible adult can grant access on your behalf.

If we discover hidden issues (rot, water damage, structural problems), we stop work immediately and inform you. We'll show you the problem, explain your options, and provide a quote for any additional work required. We get your written approval before proceeding with anything outside the original scope. No surprise charges.

Plumbing & electrical

Included: Reconnecting existing plumbing (sink, dishwasher connections). Not included: Moving pipes, installing new water points, gas connections, or any alterations requiring a licensed plumber. If plumbing alterations are needed, we can recommend qualified plumbers.

Included: Reconnecting existing appliance plugs (oven, hob, extractor). Not included: Installing new electrical points, moving outlets, or any wiring changes. Electrical alterations must be done by a certified electrician for safety and legal compliance.

We will: Create cutouts and spaces for built-in appliances, install built-in ovens and microwaves into cabinetry, and connect existing appliance plugs to outlets. We won't: Install gas hobs (requires a licensed gas fitter), do new electrical wiring, or handle appliance warranties. Appliances must be on-site before installation begins.

Costs & payment

No. Our quotes are fully itemised — materials (with markup explained), labour, transport, demolition (if applicable), and sundries. The only additional costs would be unforeseen issues discovered during work, and we always get your approval before proceeding with any extras.

Cabinet pricing depends entirely on your project's linear meterage and the materials you choose. Giving a phone estimate would mean giving you a guess — which protects neither of us. We'd rather give you an accurate price after seeing your actual space.

We work with a standard 50/40/10 payment structure. For larger projects or special circumstances, adjusted payment schedules can be discussed. We don't offer formal financing, but we can discuss phasing the project to spread costs over time.

Warranty & after-sales

Minor adjustments are normal as hinges and runners settle during the first few weeks. This is covered under warranty — just contact us and we'll come back to make the adjustments at no charge.

We're available for repairs and adjustments after the warranty period (charged at an hourly rate). Minor tweaks like hinge adjustments may be done at no charge depending on the circumstances. We want you to be happy with your cabinetry long-term.
